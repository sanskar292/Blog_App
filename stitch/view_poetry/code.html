The Echo of Ocre

by Elena Thorne

Published Autumn Issue, 2024
Reading Time 4 Minutes

The light comes horizontal now,
a heavy pour of liquid gold
resting against the rim of the world.
It speaks in shadows long and thin,
stretched across the dry, salted earth.

We did not ask for the silence,
only the grace to hold it without flinching.
There is a weight to the dust here,
a memory of rain that never arrived,
settled deep in the lines of our palms.

Listen to the way the wind threads
through the hollow stalks of wheat.
It is a whistle, a low vibration,
the sound of a country holding its breath
before the first star breaks the veil.

If you reach for the horizon,
expect your fingers to come back stained.
Not with blood, but with the rust of time,
the slow oxidation of everything
we thought was permanent.
