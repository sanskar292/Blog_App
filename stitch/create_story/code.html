Chapter II Echoes in the Hall

It began as a whisper, no louder than the rustle of dry leaves against a cobblestone path. The city was still, caught in that fragile moment before dawn when the light is neither blue nor grey, but a secret shared between the earth and the sky.

Elias adjusted the weight of his satchel. The leather was worn thin, a map of his own restless journeys. He had come to the Archive not for gold or glory, but for a single line of poetry lost to the fires of the Third Age.
